Trivandrum Famous Places

1. Agasthyakoodam

Perched at an altitude of 1,868 meters within the Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Agastya Mala Peak is also called Agastyarkoodam. The peak features a life sized statue of Lord Agastya - a Hindu Sage. Trekking is the best way to scale the heights, and the end result is absolutely exhilarating.

2. Neyyar Dam and Wildlife sanctury

Famous for its Lion and Deer Safari, Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary is a treat for nature enthusiasts. The park houses a crocodile farm and elephant rehabilitation centre.

3. Zoological Park

Hailed as the oldest zoo in India, this The Zoological Park is famous for its various bird species brought from Ethiopia & Australia. Visitors can also take a boat ride in the lake while exploring the nature''s beauty.

4. Happy Land Water Theme Park

Happy Land Water Theme Park is quite popular among children as well as adults for its various kinds of adventurous water rides.

5. Napier Museum

Renowned for its fine collection of sculptures, paintings and musical instruments, the Napier Museum serves as a prime attraction of the city.

Thiruvananthapuram

Evergreen City of India.

Formerly known as Trivandrum, Thiruvananthapuram is the capital of the southern Indian state of Kerala. It’s the gateway to the beautiful tourist attractions of the God’s Own Country. The charming town serves as the getaway to beaches, lush hills, backwaters, wildlife sanctuaries and islands. While serving as the base for other locations, the city too has its own charm. Dotted with interesting architecture, spiritual sites, amazing beaches of its own, Ayurveda centres, shopping hubs and so much more. For a Kerala holiday, Trivandrum works as your point of arrival or departure. But, even Trivandrum tours serves as a fascinating getaway.

Built upon seven hills, Trivandrum has a huge variety of tourist attractions.
